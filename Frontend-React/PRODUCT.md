# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Drivers in Tunisia reporting a road accident immediately after it happens. They may be stressed, outdoors in bright light, holding a phone with one hand, and speaking French, Arabic, Tunisian dialect, or English. Their job is to describe what happened naturally while Constaty organizes the information required for a reliable Tunisian constat.

## Product Purpose

Constaty improves claim quality at the moment information is created. The current surface is one mobile, voice-first conversation that checks safety, listens to the driver, reviews the transcript, shows what was understood, and asks one missing or uncertain question at a time. Success means the driver can progress without feeling like they are completing a long insurance form.

## Brand Personality

Calm, trustworthy, precise. The assistant is emotionally aware without being childish, locally relevant without stereotypes, and confident without sounding accusatory or bureaucratic.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a digitized PDF, step wizard, insurance questionnaire, dense claims dashboard, futuristic AI console, chatbot with a robot avatar, generic blue-purple AI product, glassmorphic concept, or card-heavy SaaS template. Avoid decorative AI magic, glowing orbs, excessive pills, nested cards, huge marketing headings, and ornamental motion.

## Design Principles

1. Safety and reassurance come before data collection.
2. Listening is the primary interaction; typing remains an accessible fallback.
3. Ask only one clear question at a time and explain uncertainty neutrally.
4. Make the system's understanding visible and editable before it becomes claim data.
5. Keep the interface quiet enough to disappear into the driver's task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Use high contrast suitable for daylight, 16px minimum body text, large one-handed touch targets, visible keyboard focus, semantic status announcements, reduced-motion support, and complete RTL behavior. Never rely on color alone for confirmed, missing, warning, recording, or error states.
